What's the 411?

Before CDs and long before Napster, iTunes, and Spotify hit the scene, we all rocked out to our favorite cassette tapes. Although we’re living in the digital age now, let’s never forget how it felt to be an original music curator. Channeling our musical superpowers to create the perfect mix of Nirvana, Boyz II Men, and TLC, we were ultimate mixtape masters.

Much like that magical feeling of closing our eyes and cranking our boomboxes all the way up, amazing things can happen when creatives mix and mingle. That’s how the Creative Mixtape — a networking event for Baltimore creatives hosted by Charm City’s flyest groups — was born. Put your Netflix plans on pause and join us for the ultimate mixer on Tuesday, December 13. Don’t miss out or you’ll be totally buggin’.

Find out more about the Creative Mixtape event taking place on December 13, 2016, at the Horseshoe Casino on Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter with our hashtag: #CreativeMixtape.

Join the Party

AAFB

AAF Baltimore is the premier organization for communications professionals in the B-more area. Our mission is to promote and support the thriving local advertising and marketing community through webinars, legislative monitoring, networking events, awards competitions, recruitment assistance, and more.

AIGA Baltimore

AIGA is the profession's oldest and largest professional membership organization for design. We advance design as a professional craft, strategic advantage, and vital cultural force, and we work to enhance the value and deepen the impact of design across all disciplines on business, society, and our collective future.

AMA Baltimore

The American Marketing Association's Baltimore Chapter is Maryland's leading provider of networking, educational programming, and resources for marketing professionals. AMA Baltimore provides extensive opportunities for marketers to expand their networks, grow professionally, and learn about current industry trends.

B'More Creatives

B'More Creatives is a free group that connects over 1000 creative, professional women in a cohesive way that fosters growth, collaboration, and a productive learning environment. We do this through online exchange, in-person events and our mentor progam.

Girl Develop It Baltimore

Girl Develop It provides affordable hands-on workshops for adult women interested in learning web and software development in a judgment-free environment. Founded in NYC in 2010, Girl Develop It now has 53 chapters across the country. The Baltimore chapter was launched in 2014 and has over 1000 members.

PCB

PCB is a Baltimore-based organization whose mission is to inspire and educate creatives and production artists in and around Baltimore and Maryland. As the roles of creative and production has evolved, we're expanding our organization to include wider range of production industries including videographers, photographers, developers and animators.

SHAG

The Society for History and Graphics (SHAG) presents a monthly program in Baltimore to further attendees' knowledge of the history of graphic design and allied arts including illustration, architecture, book arts, and photography. Programs are free and open to anyone.

Code of Conduct

PURPOSE

The planning committee behind Creative Mixtape is dedicated to providing a harassment-free community for everyone, regardless of gender, gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, or religion. We expect all attendees to show respect and courtesy to other attendees at the Creative Mixtape. Sexual language and imagery is not appropriate. Creative Mixtape attendees violating these rules may be expelled from the event without a refund at the discretion of the planning committee.

HARASSMENT

Harassment includes offensive verbal comments related to gender, gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, or religion; sexual images in public spaces; deliberate intimidation; stalking; following; harassing photography or recording; sustained disruption at the event; inappropriate physical contact; and unwelcome sexual attention. We do not tolerate harassment in any form. All communication should be appropriate for a professional audience. Participants asked to stop any harassing behavior are expected to comply immediately.

PRIVACY CONCERNS

If a group photograph is taken at this event, your participation will serve as your agreement to the use of such a photograph for Creative Mixtape promotional purposes. If you are uncomfortable having your photograph shared, please let us know.

CONSEQUENCES OF UNACCEPTABLE BEHAVIOR

If a participant engages in harassing behavior, the Creative Mixtape planning committee may take any action they deem appropriate, including warning the offender or expelling them from the event without refund.

WHAT TO DO IF YOU WITNESS OR ARE SUBJECT TO UNACCEPTABLE BEHAVIOR

If you are being harassed, notice that someone else is being harassed, or have any other concerns, please find committee members Angelique Weger or Kate Lawless. They will be glad to assist you.

The Creative Mixtape planning committee will be happy to help members contact local law enforcement, provide escorts when possible and with reasonable notice, or otherwise assist those experiencing harassment to feel safe for the duration of the event. We value your attendance.

CONTACT INFORMATION

If you have any additional questions or concerns, please contact us at event@creativemixtape.com.
